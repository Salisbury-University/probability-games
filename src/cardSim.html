Purpose of the Tool

the purpose of this tool allows you to quickly simulate games so that you can gain a better understanding of which chip layouts can help you win faster, and in turn, better represent the distribution of sums that come about from rolling 2 dice. You can do this by simulating runs of the game and viewing how they perform compared to other runs where chips are placed differently.

In order to use this tool you follow these basic steps:

Setting a Distribution

The numbers on the cards represent how many chips are on a card. A card turns green once at least one chips is placed on it. Once the total number of chips across all cards adds up to 10, the simulate button will become available, and you can begin simulating runs.

  • Manually selecting a distribution with the +/- buttons on the cards. These buttons add/remove a chip from the card on which they are.
  • Press the “Randomly Place Chips” button, which will clear the cards and place chips randomly across them, totalling up to ten.
  • If you have a distribution saved, you can click on the distribution dropdown to load them.
  • Or if you have previously simulated games on the chart, you can hover over one of its bars to see which chip distribution is correlated with that performance, and click on that bar to set the cards with that distribution.

Simulating the Game

You can simulate the games by pressing the simulate button. This button becomes available when you place all 10 chips accorss the cards. By changing the “Number of Games” field to a higher number, the simulations will run more games with that chip distribution. It may take longer to run the games, but running more games makes the results less susceptible to outliers.

Saving a Distribution

Sometimes It's nice to quickly switch between distributions in order to compare their results side-by-side. Here are the steps to store and load distribtions:

  1. Saving a Distribution

    Place all 10 chips accross the cards, if you need help you can reference the Setting a Disribution Section. Once all 10 chips are placed, the "save distribution" button will become available. If there is already a distribution selected, you will have the option to overwrite the distribution. Clicking this button will save the distribution in the list.

  2. Load a distribution

    You can load a distribution using the dropdown by simply selecting the distribution you'd like to load

  3. Deleting a Distribution

    In order to delete a distribution, load the distribution you'd like to delete. The "delete distribution" button will become available, and if you press it, the distribution will be removed from the list of saved distributions.

Comparing Performance

  • The Recent Simulations Table. This table tracks information about all of the recent simulations you've performed. The most recent simulations go at the top of the chart. The Min and Max fields refer to the best and worst game (most and least rolls) in the simulation. The average field refers to the average of all of the games. The total roll is all of the rolls of all of the games in the simulation. The last field “distribution” shows where the chips were set for that simulation.
  • The average rolls chart. This chart visualizes the average roll performance of up to the last 10 recent simulations. Each bar's height represents the average number of rolls it took to finish that simulation. New simulations get added to the end of the chart, and if there are already 10 bars, the oldest bar (from the front) gets removed from the chart. In this way, it becomes easy to see how the performance changes between simulations. Note: you can hover over the bars to see which distribtion achieved that performance.
